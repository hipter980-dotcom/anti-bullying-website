Anti-Bullying Guide

What is Bullying?
Bullying is unwanted, aggressive behavior among school-aged children that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. The behavior is repeated over time, causing severe physical or psychological distress.

Types of Bullying & Their Impacts
Physical Bullying
Definition:
Pushing, hitting, tripping, taking or damaging someone else's personal belongings.
Impact on Victim:
Causes direct bodily injuries, chronic physical pain, persistent fear of school environments, and severe sleep disturbances.
Verbal Bullying
Definition:
Teasing, name-calling, insulting, or making malicious threats against someone.
Impact on Victim:
Erodes self-esteem, triggers severe academic decline, and develops long-term chronic social anxiety and insecurity.
Social Bullying
Definition:
Spreading rumors, deliberate social exclusion, or rallying others to isolate a peer.
Impact on Victim:
Leads to intense feelings of loneliness, severe depression, loss of friendship networks, and deep distrust towards peers.
Cyber Bullying
Definition:
Sending abusive text messages, rumors, or private info across social media platforms.
Impact on Victim:
Creates a feeling of inescapable danger 24/7, public humiliation, and extreme mental exhaustion.
Sexual Bullying
Definition:
Using crude remarks, inappropriate gestures, or unwelcome physical touching.
Impact on Victim:
Induces profound psychological trauma, intense shame, body image distortion, and long-term anxiety.
Covert Bullying
Definition:
Subtle behaviors that are hidden, such as toxic whispering, glaring, or turning away.
Impact on Victim:
Causes paranoia, progressive loss of self-confidence, emotional stress, and quiet psychological suffering.

Solutions & Actions

1. Verbal Response

Stay calm, speak up confidently with clear boundaries, and walk away.

2. Cyber Defense

Take screenshots to preserve evidence, block abusive accounts, and report.

3. School Reporting

Report the incident securely through the school's safe anonymous grievance channels.

4. Trusted Adults

Confide in parents, school guidance counselors, or trusted teachers.

5. Peer Support

Stand up as an active bystander, assist victims safely, and prevent isolation.

6. Legal & Hotlines

Contact local safety authorities or dial dedicated helplines (110 / 12355).
