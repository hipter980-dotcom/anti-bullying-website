Anti-Bullying Guide

What is Bullying?
Bullying is unwanted, aggressive behavior among school-aged children that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. The behavior is repeated over time, causing severe physical or psychological distress.

Types of Bullying & Their Impacts
Physical Bullying
Definition:
Pushing, hitting, tripping, taking or damaging belongings.
Impact on Victim:
Causes direct bodily injuries, chronic pain, and fear of school.
Verbal Bullying
Definition:
Teasing, name-calling, insulting, or making malicious threats.
Impact on Victim:
Erodes self-esteem and triggers severe academic decline.
Social Bullying
Definition:
Spreading rumors, social exclusion, or isolating a peer.
Impact on Victim:
Leads to intense feelings of loneliness and deep depression.
Cyber Bullying
Definition:
Sending abusive text messages or private info on social media.
Impact on Victim:
Creates inescapable danger 24/7 and public humiliation.
Sexual Bullying
Definition:
Using crude remarks, inappropriate gestures, or unwelcome touching.
Impact on Victim:
Induces profound psychological trauma and body image distortion.
Covert Bullying
Definition:
Subtle hidden behaviors, toxic whispering, or glaring.
Impact on Victim:
Causes paranoia, progressive stress, and quiet suffering.

Solutions & Actions

1. Verbal Response

Stay calm, speak up confidently with boundaries, and walk away.

2. Cyber Defense

Take screenshots to preserve evidence, block accounts, and report.

3. School Reporting

Report safely through the school's safe anonymous grievance channels.

4. Trusted Adults

Confide in parents, school guidance counselors, or trusted teachers.

5. Peer Support

Stand up as an active bystander, assist victims safely, and prevent isolation.

6. Legal & Hotlines

Contact local safety authorities or dial dedicated helplines (110 / 12355).
